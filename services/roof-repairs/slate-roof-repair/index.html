What We Repair on Slate Roofs

Slate & Surface

  • Replace broken/slipped slate with hooks or nails as appropriate
  • Blend replacement slate for color/texture consistency
  • Re-secure ridges/hips; patch small mortar failures

Waterproofing & Flashing

  • Repair/replace copper or lead flashing at walls, chimneys, skylights & pipes
  • Rebuild/clean valleys; install diverters where needed
  • Localized underlayment replacement at leak sources

Common Leak Sources on Slate Roofs

Valleys & Transitions

Debris or aged metal causes overflow and back-up—clean or rebuild as needed.

Penetrations

Skylights, chimneys, and vent pipes often need flashing rework or replacement.

Nailing & Breakage

Exposed nails and cracked slate allow water entry; we replace and seal properly.

Underlayment

Localized failure beneath slate can require targeted underlayment replacement.

FAQ

Can you repair during light rain?

We work in safe, dry windows. Temporary protection is available if the roof is actively leaking.

Do you service synthetic/slate-look products?

Yes—let us know the brand if known; we’ll verify repair approach and availability.

When is replacement better than repair?

If widespread underlayment or flashing is failing, we’ll outline repair vs. re-roof options with pros/cons and photos.
