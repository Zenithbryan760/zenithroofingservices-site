Leak Repair • Tile Lift & Relay • Underlayment Replacement

Most tile leaks aren’t the tile. Tiles shed sun and rain for decades—but the underlayment and some flashings age out and start leaking. That’s why many homes need a lift & relay (we reuse your tile, replace the membranes/flashings, then reset the tile).

FAQ

Common Questions About Tile Roof Repair

Is it better to repair a leak or do a full lift & relay?
Small, isolated leaks can often be repaired. If you’re seeing multiple leaks or your roof is ~20–30 years old on original felt, a lift & relay is usually the smarter long-term fix. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
What underlayment do you use under tile?
We recommend modern synthetics and peel-and-stick in valleys/eaves for durability and better dry-in performance compared to older organic felts. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
What is bird-stop / eave closure and weather blocking?
Factory components that close openings at the eave and ridges to deter pests and wind-driven rain, while supporting the first course of tile. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}
Do tiles themselves wear out?
Concrete/clay tiles last a very long time; most issues come from the membranes and flashings beneath. That’s why we reuse your tile and replace the underlayment. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}
