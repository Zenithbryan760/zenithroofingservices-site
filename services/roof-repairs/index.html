Our Repair Process

  1. Conversation & leak history: timing, prior attempts, attic access if available.
  2. Targeted inspection: surface, flashings, penetrations, valleys, transitions.
  3. Diagnosis with photos: you get a labeled photo set.
  4. Clear scope & price: itemized; risks/unknowns flagged up-front.
  5. Repair day: perform scope per manufacturer specs.
  6. Water test (as needed): controlled hose test to confirm.
  7. Wrap-up & warranty: completion photos + written warranty.

SoCal rule of thumb: around ~25+ years, chasing leaks often outspends a planned replacement—if that’s the smarter move, we’ll say so.

Asphalt Shingle Roof Repairs

Architectural & 3-tab shingles

Typical Repairs

  • Replace wind-damaged/missing shingles; seal tabs
  • Reflash pipe jacks, skylights, chimneys
  • Repair step/roof-to-wall flashing & crickets
  • Address nail pops, exposed fasteners, ridge vent leaks

When Repair Makes Sense

  • Isolated storm damage on a healthy roof (< 15–20 yrs)
  • Localized leaks around one penetration/transition

Consider Replacement

  • Widespread granule loss, brittle shingles, frequent blow-offs
  • Multiple leaks across different areas
  • System age near ~25+ years

Temporary fix: mastic + replacement shingles can bridge a storm; plan larger repair in dry weather.

Concrete/Clay Tile Roof Repairs

Lift & relay tiles, underlayment repairs

Typical Repairs

  • Replace cracked/slipped tiles; proper headlap
  • Open/repair valleys; replace rusted or undersized valley metal
  • Localized underlayment replacement where leaks occur
  • Reflash roof-to-wall, headwall, skylight, chimneys

Homeowner Scenarios

  • Ceiling stain near a valley after heavy rain
  • Leak where second-story wall meets patio cover roof

Repair vs. Replace

  • Tiles outlast underlayment; widespread brittle felt ⇒ choose lift & lay
  • Small areas: localized underlayment repair; recurring leaks: full underlayment

Temporary fix: emergency tile reset + valley clean-out to stop water until underlayment work.

TPO / PVC Flat Roof Repairs

Heat-welded membranes

Typical Repairs

  • Weld patches over open seams/fishmouths/punctures
  • Reflash pipes, HVAC stands, drains, curb units
  • Add walkpads; adjust scuppers/drains for ponding

When Repair Works

  • Membrane otherwise sound with isolated seam failures
  • Penetration leaks from aged flashings

Consider Replacement

  • UV chalking/brittleness; widespread seam failure
  • Wet insulation trapped beneath
  • Age near ~20–25+ years

Temporary fix: membrane-compatible tape + targeted heat-weld patches until permanent work.

Built-Up (BUR) & Modified Bitumen Repairs

Hot-mop, cap sheet, torch-down

Typical Repairs

  • Cut/patch blisters; reinforce with mod-bit
  • Seal alligatoring & splits; recoat as needed
  • Reflash drains, scuppers, metal edge transitions

Homeowner Scenarios

  • Ceiling spots after hot days then rain (blister popped)
  • Leak near drain/scupper low point

Repair vs. Replace

  • Repairs reliable if plies are sound/dry
  • Widespread cracking or moisture ⇒ new system

Temporary fix: cold-applied mastic + polyester reinforcement to stop active drips.

Silicone-Coated Roof Repairs

Coating touch-ups & re-seals

Typical Repairs

  • Re-seal penetrations, seams, terminations with silicone
  • Patch thin/worn areas; address ponding spots
  • Prep & recoat aged/chalked surfaces

When Repair Works

  • Coating mostly intact; substrate dry and sound
  • Isolated mechanical/traffic wear

Consider Replacement

  • Coating failure widely exposing substrate
  • Poor adhesion from improper prep; saturated substrate

Temporary fix: clean/dry area and apply silicone-compatible patch.

Metal Roof Repairs

Standing seam & corrugated roofs

Typical Repairs

  • Tighten/replace fasteners & neoprene washers
  • Seal end-laps & side-laps with butyl + compatible sealants
  • Reflash penetrations/curbs; replace failed boots

Homeowner Scenarios

  • Drip during wind-driven rain along panel laps
  • Rust staining at fastener rows or edges

Repair vs. Replace

  • Good: limited corrosion, isolated fastener back-out
  • Replace: widespread corrosion or structural issues

Temporary fix: replace critical fasteners + butyl-backed reinforcement at active leaks.

Metal Roof Panel Repairs

Sectional replacement & lap details

Typical Repairs

  • Replace damaged panels; correct end-lap distances
  • Re-seat closures; add foam closures where missing
  • Seal panel penetrations (solar, satellite, additions)

When to Re-panel

  • Hail/mechanical damage across large areas
  • Severe oil-canning or misalignment causing leaks

SPF (Spray Polyurethane Foam) Roof Repairs

Foam scarification & topcoat

Typical Repairs

  • Scarify damaged foam; re-foam; shape for drainage
  • Recoat where topcoat is thin/worn
  • Seal penetrations, scuppers, parapet transitions

Homeowner Scenarios

  • Topcoat worn through (yellow foam showing)
  • Cracks at transitions/equipment curbs

Repair vs. Replace

  • Effective when foam is dry and sound
  • If saturation is present, cut-out & replacement

Temporary fix: compatible coating patches to protect exposed foam.

Roof Repair FAQs

How fast can you stop an active leak?
During rain, we often provide emergency dry-in the same day. Permanent repairs follow when it’s dry and safe to work.
Do repairs come with a warranty?
Yes—workmanship warranty for the specific repair area. If replacement is the smarter spend, we’ll say so up-front.
Will you match my shingles or tiles?
We source the closest available match; weathering/discontinued lines can create slight differences—we’ll show options first.
Do small repairs extend the life of a very old roof?
Repairs can buy time, but for systems near ~25+ years, recurring fixes can outspend a planned replacement.
