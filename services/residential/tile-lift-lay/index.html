Underlayment Systems We Offer

Choose from proven **two-layer** and **peel-and-stick** assemblies designed for tile in Southern California. We’ll recommend the best fit based on pitch, exposure, and fire rating needs.

Comparison of tile underlayment systems
System Build Best For Highlights
Two-Layer “RightStart” Style Base + cap synthetic layers (e.g., Malarkey RightStart approach) General SoCal tile applications Durable, breathable, excellent under tile battens; strong nail-hold
Two-Layer “Titanium” Style Two plies of premium synthetic (Titanium-type underlayments) Hot exposures, long runs, added redundancy High tear strength, UV/work window during staging, robust overlap
Peel-and-Stick FR (Fire-Rated) Self-adhered membrane designed for tile assemblies Wildland-urban interface or where a **fire-rated** stack is preferred Self-sealing around fasteners, clean laps, enhanced fire performance

We build to manufacturer instructions and local code. Final spec may vary by roof geometry, pitch, ventilation, and inspector requirements.

Our Tile Lift & Lay Process

1) Protect & Stage

Protect landscaping/hardscape, carefully remove and stage tiles for reuse.

2) Deck Prep

Inspect decking; address damaged sheathing and prepare for new underlayment.

3) Underlayment Install

Install the chosen **two-layer** or **peel-and-stick** system with proper laps and fasteners.

4) Flashings & Valleys

Upgrade valley metal and integrate new headwall/sidewall/chimney/skylight flashings.

5) Relay Tiles

Replace broken tiles, add bird-stop/eave closure, re-bed/re-point mortar/foam where needed.

6) Final Check

Cleanup, photo documentation, and walkthrough so you know exactly what was done.

Tile Lift & Lay FAQs

Is this better than a full tile replacement?

If your tiles are in good shape, Lift & Lay targets the real failure point—underlayment and flashings—while keeping your existing tiles and look.

Will you walk on and break my tiles?

We use tile-safe paths and staging. Some breakage is normal; we replace damaged tiles and blend the repair.

Do you offer fire-rated options?

Yes—**peel-and-stick FR** systems are available for assemblies where a fire-rated stack is preferred or required.
