5-Minute DIY Leak Triage (Stay Safe & Off the Roof)

1) Protect the area

  • Move furniture and electronics. Place a bucket under drips; use a towel as a splash guard.
  • If water is near a light/ceiling fan, turn that circuit off at the breaker.

2) Note when it leaks

During hard rain? After wind? Only when sprinklers hit a wall? Timing helps pinpoint the source.

3) Look for inside clues

  • Mark the ceiling stain edge with painter’s tape and date it.
  • If safe, peek in the attic with a flashlight—look for wet decking or trails (no roof walking).

4) Check outside from the ground

  • Focus on walls & transitions: skylights, chimneys, wall flashings, valleys, vents, and where roof meets gutters.
  • Note any missing shingles/tiles or debris piled in valleys/gutters.

5) Take & send clear photos

See “Best Photos to Send” below. Photos often let us prep parts and fix faster.

Common Leak Sources We Fix

Skylights & Curbs

Failed curb flashing, old gaskets, or cracked lenses. We reseal, re-flash, or replace curb-mounted units.

Wall/Chimney Flashings

Counterflashing gaps and dried sealants create wind-driven leaks at stucco/siding transitions.

Valleys & Transitions

Debris dams and mis-aligned tiles/shingles push water sideways under laps.

Roof Vents & Pipes

UV-cracked flashings and boots let water track along pipes into the attic.

Missing/Lifted Shingles & Tiles

Wind or foot traffic can break fasteners and expose underlayment.

Flat Roof Details

Seams, ponding zones, and terminations at walls/scuppers need membrane-specific repairs.
