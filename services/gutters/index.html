5" vs 6" Seamless Gutters

5-inch K-style gutters are the standard for many homes and work well with typical roof sizes and rainfall. 6-inch K-style gutters carry more water and reduce overflow on larger roof planes, steep pitches, or where downspout runs are longer. We'll size your system based on roof area, layout, and local conditions.

Gutter Guards That Actually Help

Guard systems keep leaves and debris out so water flows where it should—away from fascia and foundations. We match the guard to your roof type and tree coverage to reduce maintenance and prevent storm overflow.

Guard Types We Offer

  • Mesh screens: Fine filtering for small debris
  • Reverse curve: Surface tension water guidance
  • Bottle brush: Allows water through while blocking debris
  • Perforated: Durable option for most environments

Gutters & Guards — FAQ

What's the difference between 5" and 6" gutters?
5" gutters fit most homes with standard rooflines and rainfall. 6" gutters carry more water, ideal for big roof areas or steep pitches.
Do aluminum gutters hold up near the coast?
Yes. Aluminum resists rust and is lightweight. With correct fasteners and sealants, it performs well in coastal Southern California.
Will guards stop all cleaning?
They drastically reduce it, but an occasional rinse/inspection keeps everything working perfectly.
How long do seamless gutters last?
With proper installation and maintenance, seamless aluminum gutters can last 20+ years in our climate.
