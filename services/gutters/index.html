5" vs 6" K-Style — Which Size Do I Need?

5" K-Style Gutters

Great for most single-family homes with standard roof areas. Often paired with 2"×3" downspouts, or 3"×4" where a bit more flow is needed.

6" K-Style Gutters

Best for larger roof planes, steep pitches, long runs, or heavy rainfall concentration. Usually installed with 3"×4" downspouts for faster drainage and fewer overflows.

Our Seamless Gutter Installation Process

  1. Assessment & layout: measure eaves, locate downspouts, confirm 5" or 6" sizing.
  2. On-site fabrication: form seamless runs to exact length; prep miters and end-caps.
  3. Mounting: hidden hangers set ~24"–32" O.C. with the correct fastener.
  4. Sealing & pitch: correct slope to outlets; seal end-caps and miters.
  5. Downspouts: size and route to grade, splash blocks, or drains as needed.
  6. Cleanup & walkthrough: magnet sweep and final quality check.

Gutter FAQ

Do I need gutter guards?

If trees are near the roof line or you get frequent clogs, guards cut maintenance and keep water moving to the ground instead of over the eave.

Will 5" work for my home?

Often yes. If you’ve had overflow on long or steep sections—or have large roof areas feeding one run—6" is the safer choice. We’ll size it during your estimate.
