1) What Type of Roof Do I Have?

Pitched (Sloped) Roofs

Looks like triangles from the street. Sheds water by gravity. Common materials:

  • ✓ Asphalt Shingles — layered tabs that overlap.
  • ✓ Tile — curved or flat pieces (concrete or clay).
  • ✓ Metal — standing seams that run vertically.
  • ✓ Slate — natural stone shingles.

Flat / Low-Slope Roofs

Looks flat from above; water drains to drains or scuppers. Common systems:

  • ✓ TPO/PVC — white sheet membranes, seams are heat-welded.
  • ✓ Torch-Down (Mod-Bit) — black/gray with granules.
  • ✓ Silicone Coating — bright white coating applied over an existing roof.
Quick ID Tip: If you can safely step back and zoom with your phone: shingles = small rectangular tabs • tile = bigger pieces, often curved • metal = long vertical ribs • white sheet = TPO/PVC • gritty gray sheet = torch-down.

2) Do I Need a Repair or a Full Replacement?

Good Repair Candidates

  • ✓ One-area leak (pipe, valley, skylight).
  • ✓ A few missing shingles/tiles.
  • ✓ Flat roof with a couple of open seams.

Replacement Is More Likely When

  • ✓ Widespread wear or many leaks.
  • ✓ Underlayment/membrane is at end of life.
  • ✓ Extensive deck damage or poor ventilation.

Age ranges vary by product and exposure, but many shingle roofs run 20–30 yrs, tile underlayment 20–30+ yrs, and single-ply membranes 15–25+ yrs with maintenance.

3) What Should I Look For Right Now?

Ceiling Stains

Brown rings indicate active or past leaks.

Missing Pieces

Shingles/tiles blown off or cracked.

Ponding Water

Flat areas holding water > 48 hours.

Debris Piles

Valleys/gutters clogged reduce drainage.

Old Skylights

Brittle domes/failed seals cause leaks.

Rust/Cracks

At metal edges or around penetrations.

4) Smart Questions to Ask Any Roofer

  • ✓ What’s the exact scope (line items) and photos of issues?
  • ✓ How will you handle flashings, ventilation, and drainage?
  • ✓ For flat roofs: are seams heat-welded or torch-applied? ES-1 edges?
  • ✓ What warranty applies (labor/material), and what voids it?
  • ✓ Will you protect landscaping and do a daily cleanup?
  • ✓ What permits are required, and who handles them?

5) Mini Glossary (Plain English)

Underlayment

Water-resistant layer under shingles/tiles; key to keeping water out.

Flashing

Metal pieces that waterproof at walls, chimneys, pipes, and edges.

Ridge Vent / Intake

Lets hot air out and cool air in; balances attic airflow on pitched roofs.

Scupper / Drain

Openings and bowls that move water off flat roofs.

Deep Dives (Optional)

Shingles vs Tile vs Metal vs Slate

Shingles are versatile & budget-friendly; tile is durable but relies on underlayment; metal sheds water quickly and can be energy-efficient; slate is long-lasting but heavier and specialized.

TPO/PVC vs Torch-Down vs Silicone

TPO/PVC use heat-welded seams (very watertight); torch-down uses an asphalt-based membrane; silicone is a restoration coating for eligible roofs, extending life without full tear-off.
